## Governance & Business Model (from Whitepaper v0.3)

Business 


Tokenomics


Iris is a web3 first project. This means we will attempt to do everything in the fashion of other successful oracle projects and strive to provide the most decentralized, permissionless service possible.


Similar to Chainlink’s token model, the plan is to mint 100 Billion IRIS tokens. We will hold an IDO in the future and sell around 35 Billion to raise the initial funds we need for our project. Then 30 Billion will be held for the team/project and the remaining 35 Billion will go towards unminted Iris tokens to be minted and rewarded to Iris oracle nodes. 


The Iris token will be used similar to how LINK is used today in Chainlink's oracle networks. In order for nodes to partake in the Iris' DON, they will have to acquire and stake some IRIS. The nodes will also be paid for the services in the form of IRIS tokens, again how Chainlink does. One thing this crypto economic system does is create demand for our token, which in turn gives worth to the team’s IRIS tokens. 


In addition to an IDO, we would also be willing to create private rounds of fundraising using the IRIS token. In this scenario, a private investor would receive the rights to a portion of IRIS tokens to be minted at a future IDO or centralized exchange token listing. 


The IRIS token represents one way that we may raise capital. Though IRIS will always be an integral part of the DON and the Iris Protocol, an IDO isn’t the only way our team can raise capital.


Equity


A company called, Iris Protocol LLC, will be formed to represent the group of people developing Iris and related dapps. 


For context, the organization that founded Chainlink is called Smart Contract. They were founded in 2014 - much before Chainlink had an actual product. Today, the technology behind Chainlink seems to now be created by Chainlink Labs, the successor so-to-speak of the firm Smart Contract. In a similar vein, Iris Protocol LLC, will be incorporated as a for-profit business since we will be looking to appreciate the value of its owner’s shares. However this will not detract from our mission of creating open-source technology for others to iterate and build upon.


The purpose of creating a company to represent the Iris protocol and related technologies is to create another avenue to secure investment. It is no secret that crypto markets are volatile and that is especially true for new tokens. In addition to this all world markets, crypto included, have suffered recently. Therefore, it may be unwise to fundraise publicly in an IDO in a bear market and equity could prove to be a tool to fundraise capital.


In the end I hope to spin-off the Iris Protocol to the community; similar to other open source projects like Celo, Aave and Maker DAO. The decentralized nature of these new community-based organizations makes the technology they build, and care after, more resilient to attack or mismanagement. Ultimately DAOs will prove to be enduring forms of governance for projects, such as the Iris Protocol, and many more things to come.
